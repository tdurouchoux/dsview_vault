## Summary

The **Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA)** is a proposed EU regulation to strengthen Europe’s digital sovereignty and competitiveness in cloud and AI. Key objectives include accelerating AI adoption, scaling data center capacity, and reducing reliance on non-EU providers.

**Core Focus Areas:**
1. **Research & Innovation:**
   - Supports next-gen cloud/AI tech (frontier, industrial, physical AI).
   - Introduces "grand challenges" for R&D.
   - National strategies and AI centers to drive adoption.

2. **Capacity Building:**
   - Triples EU data center capacity in 5–7 years.
   - Simplifies permitting, improves access to energy/land/financing.
   - Ensures sufficient computing power for AI/data-intensive apps.

3. **Autonomy & Sovereignty:**
   - Single EU-wide framework for cloud/AI sovereignty (4 assurance levels).
   - Prioritizes EU-based innovation and supply chains.
   - Common procurement framework for public sector.
   - Promotes open-source solutions.

**Sovereignty Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Data processed/stored in EU.
- **Level 2:** Independence from third countries, transparent supply chains.
- **Level 3:** EU-owned/controlled providers, stricter criteria.
- **Level 4:** Full transparency/control, no third-country interference.

**Impact:** Aims to benefit businesses, researchers, and public bodies while reducing strategic dependencies.
