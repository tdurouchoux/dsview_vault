## Summary

Google DeepMind has launched Weather Lab to enhance tropical cyclone predictions using AI. The lab features a new experimental AI model based on stochastic neural networks, capable of predicting cyclone formation, track, intensity, size, and shape across 50 potential scenarios for up to 15 days. In collaboration with the U.S. National Hurricane Center (NHC), this model has shown accuracy comparable or superior to traditional physics-based methods.

The model incorporates vast reanalyses and cyclone-specific datasets, improving predictive abilities for both cyclone track and intensity. Internal evaluations indicate a significant improvement in prediction accuracy, outpacing leading models like ECMWF’s ENS and NOAA’s HAFS.

Weather Lab provides users with live and historical cyclone predictions and allows comparison of AI and physics-based models, aiming to better prepare communities for cyclones. While still in development, these predictions serve as research tools rather than official forecasts. The initiative focuses on gathering feedback to refine AI technology for weather forecasting and disaster preparedness.
