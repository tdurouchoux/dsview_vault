## Summary

The introduction of "Design Patterns in Causal Inference" outlines the importance of understanding causal relationships in data analysis. It emphasizes the distinction between correlation and causation, highlighting the need for rigorous frameworks to draw valid conclusions about causal effects. Key concepts introduced include causal diagrams, counterfactuals, and causal models, which serve as foundational tools for identifying and testing causal hypotheses. The introduction sets the stage for exploring various design patterns that facilitate effective causal inference, aiming to equip researchers and practitioners with techniques to improve their analysis of complex data scenarios.
