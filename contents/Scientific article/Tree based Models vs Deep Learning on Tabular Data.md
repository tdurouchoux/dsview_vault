## Summary

The paper investigates why tree-based models, such as XGBoost and Random Forests, continue to outperform deep learning models on typical tabular data. It presents extensive benchmarks comparing various deep learning methods and tree-based models across 48 datasets with distinct characteristics of tabular data. The findings indicate that tree-based models excel on medium-sized datasets (around 10,000 samples) and are faster in training.

The authors explore the differences in inductive biases between tree-based models and neural networks, identifying key challenges for developing neural networks suited for tabular data: robustness to uninformative features, preservation of data orientation, and the ability to learn irregular functions. The paper aims to stimulate research in this area by providing a standard benchmark and raw data from a comprehensive hyperparameter search for each model.
