## Summary

TabArena introduces a living benchmark for tabular data ML, addressing static benchmark issues with continuous updates. Key contributions include: (1) Manual curation of 51 representative datasets from 1053 surveyed, ensuring IID, real-world tasks, and proper licensing. (2) Benchmarking 16 state-of-the-art models (including 3 foundation models) with ~25M runs, highlighting the importance of validation methods and ensembling. (3) Public leaderboard with reproducible code and maintenance protocols. Key findings: deep learning models (TabM, RealMLP) match or outperform GBDTs with sufficient tuning/ensembling; tabular foundation models excel on small datasets; cross-model ensembles advance SOTA but may overfit validation data. The benchmark emphasizes robust evaluation protocols, rich metadata sharing, and community-driven maintenance to ensure reliability and relevance. Limitations include hardware-dependent results, strict dataset criteria reducing statistical power, and potential dataset contamination risks. Future work aims to expand to non-IID data, larger datasets, and additional tasks.
