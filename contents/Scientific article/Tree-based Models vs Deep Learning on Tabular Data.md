## Summary

The paper investigates why tree-based models, such as XGBoost and Random Forests, continue to outperform deep learning models on typical tabular data. It presents extensive benchmarks comparing various deep learning methods and tree-based models across 48 datasets with distinct characteristics of tabular data. Key findings indicate that tree-based models excel on medium-sized datasets (around 10,000 samples) and are faster in training. The authors explore the inductive biases of both model types, identifying challenges for developing neural networks tailored for tabular data, including robustness to uninformative features, preservation of data orientation, and the ability to learn irregular functions. The study aims to stimulate research in tabular-specific neural network architectures by providing a standard benchmark and extensive hyperparameter search data.
