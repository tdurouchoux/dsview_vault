## Summary

This study presents a quantitative methodology for detecting information manipulation techniques, specifically Copy-Pasta, Rewording, and Translation, utilized in foreign information campaigns on social media. The introduced “3∆-space duplicate methodology” quantifies messages based on semantic, grapheme, and language distances to identify closely related messages indicative of coordinated efforts.

Key technical points include:

1. **Method Validation**: The methodology was validated on a synthetic dataset generated using ChatGPT and DeepL, as well as a real-world dataset from Twitter related to Venezuelan actors. It effectively identified duplications across political, commercial, and entertainment messages.

2. **Focus on Message Clusters**: Instead of examining individual messages, this approach emphasizes clustered alterations to enhance detection efficiency for large-scale manipulations, including those generated by AI.

3. **Detection Dimensions**:
   - **Semantic Distance**: Measures the meaning equivalence of messages.
   - **Language Distance**: Identifies if messages are in the same language.
   - **Grapheme Distance**: Assesses textual similarity, categorizing minor changes as Copy-Pasta and significant shifts as Rewording.

4. **Algorithmic Analysis**: Several algorithms for measuring grapheme distances (e.g., Levenshtein, Ratcliff-Obershelp) were compared, with Levenshtein selected for its performance and efficiency.

5. **Cluster Analysis**: Findings from the Twitter dataset revealed different account typologies employing varied strategies, highlighting patterns of coordinated inauthentic behavior.

6. **Broad Implications**: The methodology aids in analyzing translation manipulations and addresses challenges posed by generative AI models, establishing a framework for future research in identifying similar duplicates across social networks. 

Overall, this research contributes valuable insights into the mechanics of information manipulation and provides tools for further investigations in the context of social media.
