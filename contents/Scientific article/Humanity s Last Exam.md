## Summary

Humanity's Last Exam (HLE) is an advanced benchmark designed to evaluate the capabilities of large language models (LLMs) in a multi-modal context. It features 2,700 challenging questions across various subjects, created by nearly 1,000 contributors from over 500 institutions worldwide. While existing benchmarks have become saturated, HLE aims to provide a more rigorous assessment of LLMs, as current models achieve low accuracy on its questions.

Key points include:
- HLE is intended to measure expert-level academic capabilities and is the last of its kind for closed-ended questions.
- The dataset is publicly available, but a private test set is maintained to prevent overfitting.
- Current LLMs show significant room for improvement, with low accuracy rates indicating a gap between their performance and expert knowledge.
- Calibration of models is essential, as many provide confident but incorrect answers, highlighting issues of confabulation and hallucination.
- The benchmark serves as a reference for assessing AI progress, informing discussions on development, risks, and governance.

The initiative is still accepting contributions, but new submissions are not eligible for the prize pool.
