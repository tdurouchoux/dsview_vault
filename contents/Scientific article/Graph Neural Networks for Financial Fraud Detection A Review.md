## Summary

This review paper examines the application of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in financial fraud detection, addressing the increasing complexity of financial transactions due to globalization and technology. The authors propose a unified framework categorizing existing GNN methodologies, emphasizing their ability to capture intricate relational dynamics within financial networks. Key findings suggest that GNNs significantly outperform traditional fraud detection methods. The paper reviews over 100 studies, identifying both the strengths of GNNs in real-world applications and the gaps in current research, while suggesting potential future research directions to improve GNN deployment in financial systems.
