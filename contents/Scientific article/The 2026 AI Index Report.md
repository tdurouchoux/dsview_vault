## Summary

The 2026 AI Index Report by Stanford HAI highlights key trends and insights in AI development and adoption. Key takeaways include:

- AI capability is accelerating, with industry leading 90% of frontier models. Performance on coding benchmarks (e.g., SWE-bench Verified) improved from 60% to near 100% in a year, and 88% of organizations have adopted AI.
- The U.S.-China AI model performance gap has closed, with models from both countries trading leads. The U.S. leads in top-tier models and patents, while China leads in publications and industrial robotics.
- The U.S. dominates AI data centers (5,427, 10x more than any other country) and relies heavily on TSMC for AI chip fabrication.
- AI shows "jagged frontier" performance: excelling in complex tasks (e.g., IMO gold medal) but struggling with basic tasks (e.g., reading analog clocks at 50.1% accuracy).
- Responsible AI lags behind capability growth, with safety benchmarks underreported and AI incidents rising to 362 in 2025.
- The U.S. leads in AI investment ($285.9B) but faces a decline in global AI talent attraction (89% drop since 2017).
- Generative AI adoption reached 53% globally within three years, faster than PCs or the internet, with significant economic value ($172B annually in the U.S.).
- Education systems lag in AI integration, though informal learning is growing. AI sovereignty is a key policy focus, with open-source development redistributing participation.
- Public and expert opinions on AI’s impact diverge sharply (73% of experts vs. 23% of the public see positive job impacts), and trust in AI regulation varies globally.

The report covers 9 chapters: R&D, Technical Performance, Responsible AI, Economy, Science, Medicine, Education, Policy & Governance, and Public Opinion.
