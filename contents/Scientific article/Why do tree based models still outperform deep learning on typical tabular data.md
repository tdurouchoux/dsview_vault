## Summary

Tree-based models, such as XGBoost and Random Forests, outperform deep learning on typical tabular data due to their superior performance on medium-sized datasets (around 10K samples) and faster computation times. Extensive benchmarks were conducted across 48 datasets, revealing that tree-based methods maintain a state-of-the-art status. The research identifies key challenges for designing effective neural networks for tabular data: robustness to uninformative features, preservation of data orientation, and the ability to learn irregular functions. The study aims to stimulate further research by providing benchmarks and data from extensive hyperparameter searches.
