## Summary

The article discusses a novel approach to weather prediction via a machine learning model called Aardvark Weather, which replaces the traditional numerical weather prediction (NWP) pipeline. Key findings include:

- Aardvark Weather processes observational data and generates both global and local forecasts without relying on numerical systems during deployment.
- The model achieves higher accuracy than traditional NWP baselines for multiple weather variables and can provide skillful local forecasts up to ten days in advance.
- End-to-end tuning enhances the accuracy of local forecasts, showcasing that effective forecasting is achievable solely through data-driven methods.
- This approach promises significant reductions in computational costs and enables the rapid development of customized weather models for various users, marking a significant advance in the field of atmospheric science and machine learning applications.
