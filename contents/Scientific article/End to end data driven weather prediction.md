## Summary

The article discusses a novel end-to-end data-driven weather prediction system called Aardvark Weather, which utilizes machine learning to enhance numerical weather prediction (NWP). Traditional NWP relies on numerical solvers, but Aardvark Weather replaces the entire NWP pipeline with a single machine learning model that processes observations to generate both global and local forecasts. The system demonstrates superior performance compared to existing operational NWP baselines across various variables and lead times, achieving skillful local forecasts up to ten days ahead. The findings suggest that effective forecasting can be achieved without traditional NWP, potentially reducing computational costs significantly and facilitating the rapid development of customized forecasting models for diverse users.
