## Summary

The repository "FriendsDon'tLetFriends" by Chenxin Li discusses poor practices in data visualization and provides guidelines for better alternatives. Key points include:

1. **Bar Plots for Mean Separation**: Avoid using bar plots for comparing means due to potential misrepresentation of data distributions.
2. **Violin Plots for Small Samples**: Violin plots are ineffective with small sample sizes as they can mislead interpretations of distribution.
3. **Bidirectional Color Scales**: Use unidirectional color scales for unidirectional data to avoid confusion.
4. **Bar Plot Meadows**: Multi-factorial data should be presented thoughtfully rather than in cluttered bar plots.
5. **Heatmaps**: Always reorder rows and columns for clarity and check for outliers to avoid misinterpretation.
6. **Data Range Checks**: Ensure to check the range of data at each factor level to avoid overlooking significant effects.
7. **Network Graph Layouts**: Experiment with different layouts for network graphs to enhance interpretability.
8. **Position vs. Length**: Distinguish between position-based and length-based visualizations to prevent misleading representations.
9. **Pie and Donut Charts**: Avoid pie charts due to difficulty in comparing angles; prefer stacked bar plots for clarity.
10. **Concentric Donuts**: These can misrepresent data; use simpler visualizations instead.
11. **Color Scales**: Avoid red/green and rainbow color scales due to accessibility issues; use colorblind-friendly alternatives.
12. **Reordering Stacked Bar Plots**: Optimize the order of stacked bars for better clarity.
13. **Mixing Visualizations**: Avoid combining different types of visualizations that serve distinct purposes, as it can lead to confusion.

The content emphasizes the importance of thoughtful design in data visualization to enhance clarity and accuracy.
