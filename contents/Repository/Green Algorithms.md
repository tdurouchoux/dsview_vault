## Summary

The Green Algorithms project focuses on promoting environmentally sustainable computational science. Key components include a carbon footprint calculator to help researchers estimate the carbon emissions of their computational work, tips for sustainable computing practices, and a specialized tool for calculating the carbon output of computations on high-performance computing (HPC) platforms.

Recently, the project received the 2024 Susannah Boddie Impact of the Year Award for its significant influence on policy and its role in raising awareness about carbon footprint estimation in computational research. A new publication outlines the GREENER principles for sustainable computational science, providing foundational guidelines and best practices. Additionally, a Community of Practice is being formed to engage individuals interested in environmentally sustainable computational methods.
