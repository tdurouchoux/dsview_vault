## Summary

The course CS 329S focuses on designing machine learning systems that are deployable, reliable, and scalable. It emphasizes an iterative framework that considers stakeholder objectives, data management, feature engineering, model selection, and the human aspects of ML projects, including privacy and fairness.

Key logistics include:
- Lectures are held twice a week, with a mix of lectures and tutorials.
- Grading consists of a final project (65%), short assignments (30%), and participation (5%).
- Office hours are available for student support.

Prerequisites include basic computer science knowledge, familiarity with machine learning algorithms, and experience with frameworks like TensorFlow or PyTorch. The course promotes collaboration while adhering to a strict honor code regarding academic integrity.

The course materials are designed to be hands-on, with a focus on real-world applications. Students are encouraged to engage actively and can audit the course if they meet specific criteria. All course resources, including lecture notes and assignments, will be publicly available.
