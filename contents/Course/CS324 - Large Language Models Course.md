## Summary

The CS324 course focuses on Large Language Models (LLMs) in natural language processing (NLP), emphasizing their modeling, theory, ethics, and practical applications. Key topics include the capabilities and challenges of LLMs, ethical considerations, and hands-on experience with models like GPT-3 and BERT.

Course logistics include in-person classes with a mix of lectures and discussions, where students engage with assigned readings. Grading is based on paper reviews (20%) and two projects (80%). The projects involve evaluating and building language models, requiring collaboration in small groups and clear documentation.

Submissions are managed through Gradescope, with specific guidelines for deadlines, late submissions, and regrade requests. Students are encouraged to utilize resources like Ed for communication and support.
