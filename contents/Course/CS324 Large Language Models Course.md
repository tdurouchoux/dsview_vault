## Summary

The CS324 course focuses on Large Language Models (LLMs) in natural language processing (NLP), emphasizing their modeling, theory, ethics, and practical applications. Key topics include the capabilities and challenges of LLMs, ethical considerations, and hands-on experience with models like GPT-3 and BERT. 

The course structure includes lectures and discussions, with grading based on paper reviews (20%) and two projects (80%). Students will engage in reading and reviewing academic papers, followed by panel discussions. Projects involve evaluating and building language models, requiring collaboration in small groups and clear documentation.

Logistics include in-person classes with some remote sessions, communication through designated platforms, and submission of coursework via Gradescope. Late submissions are allowed with penalties after a certain limit, and regrade requests can be made under specific conditions.
