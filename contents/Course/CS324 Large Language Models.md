## Summary

CS324 focuses on Large Language Models (LLMs) in natural language processing (NLP). The course covers modeling, theory, ethics, and system aspects of LLMs, including hands-on experience. Classes consist of lectures and student-led discussions based on assigned readings.

Key logistics include:
- Instructors: Percy Liang, Tatsunori Hashimoto, Christopher Ré, Rishi Bommasani, and Sang Michael Xie.
- Class Structure: Lectures (45 minutes) followed by discussions (45 minutes).
- Grading: 20% paper reviews and discussions; 80% from two projects.

Coursework involves:
1. Paper reviews: Analyze assigned papers before class and participate in discussions.
2. Projects: 
   - Project 1 evaluates existing language models like GPT-3.
   - Project 2 focuses on building language models like BERT-base, emphasizing a focal property identified in Project 1.

All submissions should be via Gradescope, and students can utilize late days within specified limits. Regrade requests must be made within a week of grade release.
