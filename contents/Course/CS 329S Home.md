## Summary

The course CS 329S focuses on an iterative framework for developing deployable, reliable, and scalable machine learning systems. It addresses stakeholder objectives, design tradeoffs, and includes topics such as data management, feature engineering, model selection, system monitoring, and ethical considerations like privacy and fairness.

Logistics include lectures twice a week, a final project (65% of the grade), short assignments (30%), and participation (5%). Office hours are set for faculty and TAs, and communication is encouraged through a dedicated Piazza forum. Academic accommodations are available through the Office of Accessible Education.

Prerequisites include a background in computer science, understanding of machine learning algorithms, and familiarity with frameworks like TensorFlow or PyTorch. The course emphasizes the importance of design in machine learning to avoid outdated systems.

There's an honor code regarding academic integrity, and students can audit the course but won't receive grading support. Recorded lectures will be available, and attendance is encouraged but not mandatory. Group work is allowed for assignments and finding project partners. Overall, the course aims to provide hands-on experience in building real-world machine learning applications.
