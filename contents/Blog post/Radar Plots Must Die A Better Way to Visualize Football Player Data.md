## Summary

The blog post critiques the use of radar charts in football (soccer) analytics, highlighting their flaws in visualizing player performance metrics. Key issues include arbitrary ordering of metrics, radial encoding distortions, and difficulty in comparing non-adjacent values. The author proposes a replacement visualization—a "football player card"—comprising two panels: a composite score with additive decomposition (Panel A) and grouped percentile ridges showing peer distributions (Panel B). The new design addresses radar chart pitfalls by using linear encodings, shared axes, and meaningful groupings derived from Bayesian factor analysis. The post emphasizes that effective visualizations should encode real quantities and avoid arbitrary design choices. PyMC Labs offers services in Bayesian modeling and sports analytics systems.
