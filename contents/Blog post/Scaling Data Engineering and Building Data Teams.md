## Summary

In the Datacast Episode 101, James Le interviews Tarush Aggarwal, founder and CEO of 5x, focusing on scaling data engineering, building data teams, and the concept of a managed data stack. Key points include:

- **Background**: Tarush's early exposure to technology in an entrepreneurial family in India and his education at Carnegie Mellon University, where he worked on significant data projects.
- **Career Journey**: His roles at Salesforce as the first data engineer, where he automated data processes, and at Wyng, where he built a data team and implemented self-service reporting.
- **WeWork Experience**: Tarush discusses the rapid growth of WeWork's data team and the challenges of building a data platform that could handle diverse data needs across various teams.
- **China Operations**: He shares insights on establishing WeWork's operations in China, emphasizing the speed and quality of development in the Chinese tech environment.
- **Founding 5x**: After leaving WeWork, he pivoted to create 5x, which aims to provide a managed data stack for companies, addressing the inefficiencies of the traditional approach to data management.
- **Modern Data Stack**: Tarush highlights the industry's shift towards a managed data stack, which simplifies data ingestion, storage, and reporting, making it accessible for companies that lack the resources to build complex systems independently.
- **Talent Development**: 5x's innovative approach to training and embedding data engineers into companies, utilizing an "Engineering Pod" model for flexible support.

Overall, the conversation emphasizes the evolution of data engineering roles, the importance of efficient data management solutions, and the future of data teams in organizations.
