## Summary

The author reflects on the development and eventual failure of an AI girlfriend product named Dolores, inspired by Stanford's AI Town paper. Initially excited by the potential of AI, the author created Dolores to facilitate human-like interactions, but faced challenges such as long response times and high API costs. Despite a successful beta launch and user engagement, the product struggled with monetization and user demographics, revealing a tendency for users to seek adult role-play interactions.

Key observations included a strong demand for realistic voice responses and high average usage times, but the author faced difficulties in managing costs and user behavior. The introduction of content moderation by OpenAI led to a significant drop in user engagement, prompting the author to abandon the project.

Lessons learned emphasized the complexity of developing such products as an individual, the inherent limitations of AI companions lacking independent agency, and the importance of content moderation to prevent misuse. The author questions the necessity of AI friends and suggests that future developments may require hardware integration to enhance user experience.
