## Summary

A developer shares his experience creating an AI "girlfriend" product named Dolores, inspired by Stanford's AI Town paper. Initially launched after rapid development, Dolores aimed to offer realistic interactions through customization of characters and voice. Despite user interest, challenges arose with high operational costs and user retention, particularly with many engaging in adult role-play scenarios. 

Key observations included strong demand for realistic voices and lengthy user interactions, but the developer faced setbacks when content moderation decreased user engagement. The project's financial viability was further questioned due to uneven token usage among subscribers. Ultimately, the developer concluded that creating a meaningful AI companion is complex, especially without hardware support for a more interactive experience. 

Lessons learned highlight the importance of a robust development environment, the limitations of virtual friendships, and the need for content moderation to ensure user safety. The reflection raises questions about humanity's need for AI companionship.
