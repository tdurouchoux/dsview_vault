## Summary

The article from MIT Technology Review analyzes the energy consumption and carbon footprint of artificial intelligence (AI) technology. It highlights the significant rise in electricity usage related to AI, driven by increased demand for AI applications, leading to a doubling of data center electricity consumption between 2017 and 2023. Key points include:

1. **Energy Demands**: AI data centers now account for 4.4% of U.S. electricity consumption, projected to grow significantly by 2028. AI is expected to consume as much electricity as 22% of U.S. households.

2. **Carbon Emissions**: The carbon intensity of power used by data centers is reported to be 48% higher than the U.S. average, with trends indicating a move towards more carbon-intensive energy sources.

3. **Individual Query Impact**: While the energy cost of a single AI query appears low, it adds up due to the scale of use. The energy required varies significantly based on model size and prompt complexity.

4. **Cost and Future Planning**: Companies plan massive investments in AI infrastructure, raising concerns over potential costs transferred to consumers through higher electricity bills. Current scarcity of data makes it difficult to assess the full impact on energy grids and emissions.

5. **Lack of Transparency**: There is inadequate disclosure from AI companies about energy use and emissions, complicating efforts to forecast and manage future energy requirements effectively.

6. **Resource Shifts**: Major tech companies are exploring nuclear energy options to combat rising demands but face long timelines for implementation. Meanwhile, energy usage patterns are expected to worsen as AI evolves.

The summary emphasizes the urgent need for transparency and planning as AI continues to reshape energy consumption patterns and contribute to climate change.
