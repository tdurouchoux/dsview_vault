## Summary

To build unique, valuable, and efficient AI products, avoid common industry pitfalls. Many current AI solutions merely act as wrappers around existing models, like APIs, leading to significant issues:

1. **Lack of Differentiation**: Products that only layer on existing AI without unique technology can be quickly replicated.
2. **High Costs**: Running large language models (LLMs) is expensive. Many users find that the cost of services exceeds what they're willing to pay.
3. **Performance**: LLMs often introduce latency, making them unsuitable for applications requiring immediate responses.
4. **Limited Customization**: While fine-tuning exists, it often doesn't yield significant improvements for specific needs.

The recommended approach is to create a proprietary toolchain instead of relying on LLMs. This involves combining custom-developed models with a codebase to solve specific tasks, achieving better performance, differentiation, and cost-effectiveness.

Key practices include:
- Initially focus on traditional programming to understand the problem space.
- Identify and address gaps where specialized AI can be integrated.
- Train your own models, utilizing methods for data generation and leveraging existing frameworks for model training.

Controlling your models allows for constant improvement, greater privacy, and the ability to cater to specific user needs. This method promotes using "normal" code for core functionality, enhancing reliability while only applying AI where it truly adds value.
