## Summary

The European Parliament has approved a comprehensive AI Act aimed at regulating artificial intelligence technologies, including systems like ChatGPT. This legislation, proposed in 2021, seeks to protect citizens from potential risks associated with rapidly advancing AI while promoting innovation in Europe. The Act adopts a risk-based approach, imposing stricter requirements on higher-risk AI systems, including mandatory risk assessments and compliance checks before public release. Certain AI applications, such as predictive policing and real-time facial recognition in public spaces, are banned or heavily restricted. Companies violating the regulations could face significant fines. The law is expected to come into effect 12 months after its official publication, with most provisions requiring compliance within two years. The EU aims to establish itself as a global standard-setter for trustworthy AI amidst intense lobbying from various stakeholders.
