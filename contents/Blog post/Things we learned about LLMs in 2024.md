## Summary

In 2024, significant advancements were made in the realm of Large Language Models (LLMs). Key takeaways include:

1. **Breaking the GPT-4 Barrier**: Multiple models surpassed OpenAI's GPT-4, including Google's Gemini 1.5 Pro with extended context lengths and the ability to process video inputs.

2. **LLM Prices Decline**: Competitive forces and improved efficiency led to drastic reductions in costs for using high-performance LLMs, making them more accessible.

3. **Rise of Multimodality**: Many models adopted multi-modal capabilities, allowing interactions with images, audio, and video.

4. **Voice and Live Camera Features**: These emerging functionalities created an interactive experience previously thought to be science fiction.

5. **Prompt-Driven Application Generation**: Creating applications via prompts became commonplace, with several platforms offering integrated tools for developers.

6. **Environmental Impact**: While improvements were noted in operational efficiency, the infrastructure buildup for AI raised environmental concerns.

7. **Agents**: The development of AI "agents" remains nebulous, with unclear definitions and limited practical implementations.

8. **Importance of Evaluations**: Good evaluation systems are essential for effectively leveraging LLMs for applications.

9. **Knowledge Distribution Issues**: There is a vast gap in understanding LLM advancements between enthusiasts and the general public.

10. **Synthetic Data Utilization**: Training models with synthetic data showed promise, countering fears of model collapse from an oversaturation of AI-generated content.

The article reflects on these themes as part of an ongoing exploration of LLM capabilities and challenges, emphasizing the importance of careful consideration when deploying these powerful tools.
