## Summary

The article discusses the importance of implementing data contracts within organizations to improve communication and governance between data producers and consumers. It highlights a case where miscommunication led to overstocking due to changes in data calculation methods not being conveyed to the analytics team, demonstrating the need for documented contracts to formalize model evolutions, notify relevant parties, and manage operational impacts.

Key failure patterns in adopting data contracts include silent bypassing, passive resistance, lack of ownership, and excessive bureaucracy. To prevent these issues, a facilitating governance-as-a-service team should design contracts to streamline processes, clarify roles, and integrate contracts into existing workflows.

Successful data contract governance relies on clear role definitions, collaborative processes, and a well-structured governance framework. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration from the initial requirement gathering to the implementation and user approval phases.

Training and an enabling team are crucial for promoting best practices and ensuring successful adoption. It advocates a progressive, iterative approach, starting with pilot domains to demonstrate value and refine governance strategies.

The conclusion underscores that adopting data contracts is an ongoing journey requiring a balance between structure and flexibility, and that fostering a data-driven culture is essential for success.
