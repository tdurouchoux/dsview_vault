## Summary

AI-generated dashboards risk filling the "Dashboard Graveyard" if users skip fundamental design principles. Three iterations of dashboard generation using Claude Design revealed key pitfalls:

1. **Under-cooked prompts** (Iteration 1) produce polished but incoherent dashboards that merely tour data without answering real questions. AI tools may ask clarifying questions but won’t inherently guide users toward meaningful briefs.

2. **Overly focused prompts** (Iteration 2) can yield actionable structures (e.g., invest/maintain/retire frameworks) but lack critical data context (e.g., revenue/margin gaps) or editorial discipline (e.g., dead themes, inconsistent terminology).

3. **Over-engineered prompts** (Iteration 3) force premature conclusions by applying design guardrails without prior analytical exploration, resulting in visually impressive but rigid outputs that foreclose inquiry.

**Key takeaways**:
- AI accelerates dashboard creation but amplifies the need for human judgment in briefing, data validation, and contextual review.
- Fundamentals (audience alignment, decision focus, trustworthiness) remain critical; AI polish can mask shallow analysis.
- Analysts must validate AI outputs against domain knowledge and decision needs, ensuring dashboards serve as tools for interrogation, not just presentation.
