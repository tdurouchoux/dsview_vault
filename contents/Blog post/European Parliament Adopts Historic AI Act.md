## Summary

The European Parliament has adopted a groundbreaking AI Act aimed at regulating artificial intelligence technologies, including systems like OpenAI's ChatGPT. This legislation, passed on March 13, 2024, seeks to protect citizens from risks associated with rapidly evolving AI while promoting innovation in Europe. A risk-based approach is central to the regulations, imposing stricter requirements on riskier AI applications, including high-risk systems needing thorough risk assessments. Key provisions include bans on predictive policing, the use of biometric data to infer personal characteristics, and real-time facial recognition in public without judicial approval.

The Act has garnered significant bipartisan support, with 523 lawmakers voting in favor and only 46 against. EU member states are set to endorse the law in April, with full implementation expected within two years. Violations may incur hefty fines, but the act is also influenced by extensive lobbying from tech firms. Despite these pressures, European officials emphasized a commitment to maintaining a balanced and future-proof regulatory framework.
