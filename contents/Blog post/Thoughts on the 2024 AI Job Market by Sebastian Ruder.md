## Summary

The article discusses the evolving AI job market, specifically in natural language processing (NLP), and highlights several trends:

1. **Shift to Applied Research**: Research has become more application-focused, narrowing the gap between fundamental and applied research due to advancements in model capabilities. This leads to broader impacts from research but also challenges in justifying unproven projects.

2. **Startups as Alternatives to PhDs**: Startups are increasingly seen as viable paths for gaining experience in AI, providing hands-on skills and immediate impact, as opposed to the traditional academic route of a PhD.

3. **Reduced Openness in Research**: The AI community is experiencing a shift towards less open communication regarding model details and development, making publishing more challenging, especially as companies increasingly lock down their innovations.

4. **Concentration of Research**: Research is increasingly being centralized within large collaborative projects, resulting in large author teams and potentially sidelining smaller, independent research efforts.

5. **Increase in Opportunities**: The rise of generative AI has led to the proliferation of startups and job opportunities, although many challenges remain in applying these technologies effectively in business contexts.

Sebastian Ruder also discusses his personal motivations for joining Cohere over other options, emphasizing values of openness, a balanced work culture, and alignment with societal impact, alongside the benefits of remote work and a skilled team.
