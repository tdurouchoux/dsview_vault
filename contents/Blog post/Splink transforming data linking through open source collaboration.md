## Summary

Splink is an open-source Python package developed by a team of analysts at the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) aimed at improving data linking and deduplication across government datasets globally. Initiated in 2016, the tool addresses common data linking challenges, such as inconsistencies and duplicates, faced by large organizations, especially in the justice sector.

Key features of Splink include:
- Speed: Can link over a million records in about a minute.
- Accuracy: Supports user-defined fuzzy matching for better precision.
- Scalability: Handles datasets over 100 million records.
- Compatibility: Works across various operating systems and IT environments, including those without internet access.
- Unsupervised Learning: Requires no pre-labeled training data, utilizing an unsupervised approach for model training.
- Interactive Outputs: Offers user-friendly outputs for model diagnostics.

Since its launch, Splink has been downloaded over 9 million times and is lauded for significantly speeding up the record-linking process with improved accuracy and lower costs. It encourages collaboration through continuous feedback and contributions from users worldwide, emphasizing open-source principles. Splink's success highlights the potential of collaborative, open-source approaches in overcoming data silos within organizations.
