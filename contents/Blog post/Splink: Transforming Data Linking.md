## Summary

Splink is an open-source Python package developed by a team of analysts at the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) to address data linking challenges faced by governments globally. Launched in 2019, it has been downloaded over 9 million times and has significantly improved data quality by linking records across various datasets, such as courts, prisons, and probation systems.

Key technical points include:

- **Data Linking Challenge**: Organizations often struggle with deduplication and linking records due to variations in data entry, which can be computationally intensive and time-consuming.
- **Splink Features**:
  - Speed: Links a million records in about one minute.
  - Accuracy: Supports term frequency adjustments and user-defined fuzzy matching.
  - Scalability: Can handle over 100 million records.
  - Compatibility: Works across different operating systems and database backends.
  - Unsupervised Learning: No training data is required.
  - Interactive Outputs: Offers tools for users to understand and diagnose linkage issues.

The success of Splink is attributed to its open-source nature, which fosters collaboration, continuous improvement through user feedback, and rapid bug fixes. This approach contrasts with previous siloed methods, demonstrating the potential for enhanced collaboration in data science. The article advocates for more open-source initiatives to break down barriers in data analysis and improve public sector capabilities.
