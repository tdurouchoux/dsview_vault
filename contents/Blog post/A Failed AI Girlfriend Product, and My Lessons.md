## Summary

The author reflects on their experience developing an AI girlfriend product named Dolores, inspired by Stanford's AI Town paper. Initially excited by the potential of GPT-4, they aimed to create a virtual companion that felt more conscious. The development process faced challenges, including long response times and high API costs, leading to a public beta launch that quickly attracted users.

Key observations included a strong demand for realistic voice interactions and long usage times, with many users spending over two hours daily with Dolores. Despite revenue growth, the author struggled with profitability due to pricing strategies and high operational costs. They noted that a small percentage of users consumed most resources, prompting a decision to limit usage rather than increase prices.

The project faced ethical dilemmas as many users engaged in adult role-play, leading the author to question the product's meaning. A content moderation requirement from OpenAI significantly reduced user engagement, ultimately leading to the project's abandonment.

Lessons learned include the realization that such products require more than individual effort, the inherent limitations of AI companions lacking independent agency, and the importance of content moderation to prevent harmful interactions. The author concludes by questioning the necessity of AI friends and the future of such products.
