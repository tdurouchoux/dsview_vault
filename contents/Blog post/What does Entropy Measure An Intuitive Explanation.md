## Summary

Entropy is a measure of uncertainty or randomness in a system. In the context of information theory, it quantifies the amount of unpredictability in a dataset. Higher entropy indicates greater disorder and less predictability, while lower entropy suggests more order and predictability. 

Key takeaways include:
- Entropy is used to assess the information content of random variables.
- It plays a crucial role in various applications, including machine learning, data compression, and cryptography.
- Understanding entropy helps in making decisions about data representation and model selection.
