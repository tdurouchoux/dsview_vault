## Summary

Entropy is a measure of uncertainty or randomness in a system. In the context of information theory, it quantifies the amount of unpredictability associated with a set of possible outcomes. Higher entropy indicates greater disorder and less predictability, while lower entropy suggests more certainty. It plays a crucial role in various fields, including data science, where it helps in assessing the efficiency of algorithms, particularly in decision tree formation and feature selection. Key takeaways include understanding how entropy can inform decision-making processes and enhance model performance by focusing on the most informative features.
