## Summary

The article discusses the importance of adopting data contracts within organizations to improve data governance and communication between data producers and consumers. It highlights a case where a lack of proper communication led to overestimated sales forecasts, emphasizing that data contracts can formalize changes, notify stakeholders, and manage operational impacts.

Key points include:

- **Governance Mechanisms**: Data contracts should incorporate governance from the design phase, making it easier for teams to adopt good practices.
- **Common Failure Patterns**: Issues such as silent bypassing, passive resistance, lack of ownership, and excessive bureaucracy can hinder the effectiveness of data contracts.
- **Facilitating Body**: A governance-as-a-service team should clarify roles, establish standards, and integrate contracts into workflows to enhance collaboration and accountability.
- **Organizational Roles**: Clear role definitions are crucial for effective governance, including roles like Data Product Owner, Data Architect, and Data Quality Analyst.
- **Governance Process**: The process involves identifying needs, collaborative requirement writing, implementation, and communication, ensuring contracts remain relevant and high-quality.
- **Adoption Strategies**: Establishing an enabling team, providing training, and starting with pilot domains can facilitate the adoption of data contracts.
- **Success Monitoring**: Metrics should assess both technical and business impacts, focusing on data quality, time-to-market, and team satisfaction.

The article concludes that successful data contract governance is an ongoing journey that balances structure with flexibility, emphasizing the need for a strong data culture within organizations.
