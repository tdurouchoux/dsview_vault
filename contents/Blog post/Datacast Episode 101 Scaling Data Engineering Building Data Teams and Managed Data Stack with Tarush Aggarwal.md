## Summary

The podcast episode features a conversation between James Le and Tarush Aggarwal, founder and CEO of 5x, focusing on the evolution of data engineering and the concept of a managed data stack. Key topics discussed include Tarush’s background growing up in India with early exposure to technology, his college experience at Carnegie Mellon, and his journey at Salesforce where he became the first data engineer. He highlights the manual, early data practices and automation frameworks he developed.

Tarush discusses challenges faced while building the data team at Wyng, emphasizing the importance of self-service reporting and data modeling. When he joined WeWork, he navigated rapid growth and established data operations in China, adapting to the localized internet environment. 

In founding 5x, he aims to address the needs of companies seeking efficiently integrated data solutions, contrasting old data stack setups with the requirements of modern enterprises. The episode introduces the concept of an "engineering pod" to offer elastic data engineering talent as well as 5x’s strategy to partner with leading vendors for a comprehensive data stack. 

Overall, the discussion provides insights into scaling data teams, the necessity for managed data stacks, and the evolution of data engineering practices.
