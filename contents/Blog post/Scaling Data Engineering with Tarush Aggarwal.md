## Summary

In the Datacast Episode 101, James Le interviews Tarush Aggarwal, founder and CEO of 5x, focusing on scaling data engineering, building data teams, and the concept of a managed data stack. Key points include:

- **Background**: Tarush grew up in an entrepreneurial family in India, developed a passion for computer science, and studied at Carnegie Mellon University, where he worked on early Hadoop projects.
- **Career Path**: He joined Salesforce as its first data engineer, where he automated data processes and improved analytics efficiency. He later built a data team at Wyng, focusing on self-service reporting and data modeling.
- **WeWork Experience**: At WeWork, he scaled the data team significantly and established operations in China, adapting to the unique challenges of the Chinese market.
- **Founding 5x**: After a brief sabbatical, he founded 5x to address the need for a managed data stack, which simplifies data management for companies by providing an integrated platform.
- **Modern Data Stack**: Tarush emphasizes the importance of a cohesive data stack for businesses to efficiently analyze customer data and optimize operations, contrasting the traditional fragmented approach.
- **Training and Talent**: 5x focuses on training data engineers and offers an "Engineering Pod" model, providing flexible, on-demand data engineering support to companies.

Overall, the conversation highlights the evolution of data engineering roles, the challenges of scaling data teams, and the emerging need for integrated data solutions in modern enterprises.
