## Summary

The article outlines a mental model for running data science projects, drawing from the author's experience at AWS and Amazon. Key points include:

1. **Defining the Business Problem**: Start by ensuring the business problem is well-defined. Vague problems can lead to ineffective projects. Concrete examples of well-defined problems are crucial.

2. **Stakeholder Engagement**: Identifying stakeholders is essential. Often, business leaders do not present clear problems, so individual contributors may need to take the lead in scoping projects.

3. **Project Scoping**: Assess the potential impact and ROI of projects. Consider existing solutions and ensure stakeholders are committed to investing resources, including data access and support.

4. **Metrics and Success Definition**: Clearly define what success looks like and establish metrics for evaluation. Involve stakeholders in this process to ensure alignment and commitment.

5. **Execution**: This phase is generally easier if the previous steps are well-managed. Flexibility in execution is important, and regular check-ins can help track progress and realign with stakeholders.

The author emphasizes the importance of a structured approach while remaining open to different perspectives and methodologies.
