## Summary

The article discusses a mental model for running data science projects based on the author’s experience at AWS and Amazon. Key points include:

- **Defining the Business Problem**: A clear understanding of the business problem is essential. Often, organizations fail to articulate specific needs, leading to vague project scopes.

- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Identify relevant stakeholders and understand their expectations to avoid misalignment on project goals.

- **Project Scoping**: Assess the potential impact and ROI of the project. Determine the availability of existing solutions and secure commitment from stakeholders regarding resources and data access.

- **Metrics and Success Definition**: Establish clear metrics and define what success looks like for the project. This includes baseline metrics and plans for experimentation, ensuring stakeholder involvement in this process.

- **Execution**: Focus on delivering the project with flexibility in execution strategies. Regular check-ins and prototypes can facilitate alignment with stakeholders and adjustments as needed.

The author emphasizes that these processes create a stable foundation for successful execution, and invites feedback on the methodology.
