## Summary

The EU is moving towards regulating generative AI tools like ChatGPT and Stable Diffusion, shifting from vague ethical guidelines to concrete regulations. The upcoming AI Act will require companies to disclose how their models are built and trained, addressing concerns about harmful content and biased outcomes. Companies could face fines of up to 6% of their global turnover for non-compliance. The regulation aims to enhance transparency and accountability in AI development, particularly for general-purpose AI systems that can be used in various applications, including customer service and content generation.

Generative AI is recognized as a transformative technology, with predictions for 2023 indicating advancements in AI capabilities, including multilingual image generation and applications in diverse fields like architecture and pharmaceuticals. The article also highlights ongoing developments, such as Microsoft integrating ChatGPT into its products and Apple's launch of AI-narrated audiobooks, which raises concerns among artists about job displacement. Overall, the regulatory landscape is evolving to ensure responsible AI use while fostering innovation.
