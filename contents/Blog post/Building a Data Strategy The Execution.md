## Summary

Key takeaways from "Issue #58 – Building a Data Strategy (The Execution)":

1. **Execution is Critical**: Many data strategies fail not due to poor direction or lack of buy-in, but because execution isn't properly planned or prioritized. Avoid becoming a "ticket taker" by aligning work with strategic goals.

2. **Four Pillars of a Successful Data Strategy**:
   - **Vision & Strategic Pillars**: Inspiring statement and tangible breakdown of goals.
   - **Use Cases**: Specific initiatives translating strategy into outputs.
   - **Capability Assessment**: Evaluates current maturity across five domains (Business & Data Foundations, Data Engineering & Architecture, Data Management & Governance, Value Realization & Adoption) using a 1-5 or RAG rating system.
   - **Executional Roadmap**: Sequenced, owned, funded plan with clear accountability, built collaboratively with stakeholders.

3. **Capability Assessment**:
   - Assess organizational maturity against strategic needs.
   - Evaluate data architecture (tools, data flows, modeling) to identify dependencies and gaps.
   - Focus on AI-enabling capabilities and data governance.

4. **Roadmap Essentials**:
   - Prioritize use cases using a 2x2 matrix (effort vs. business value).
   - Sequence workstreams logically, defining dependencies, owners, timelines, and outcomes.
   - Ensure executive ownership, realistic timelines, and clear communication.

5. **AI Strategy Shift**: AI strategies must be more action-oriented, agile, and implemented quickly, not just theoretical use cases.

6. **Change Management**: Success hinges on bridging the gap between data and strategy, ensuring buy-in, and managing organizational change.
