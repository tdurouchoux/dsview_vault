## Summary

The article discusses the challenges of data integration in manufacturing and industrial contexts, emphasizing that effective data analysis starts with collecting and organizing data in a standardized format. Key points include:

- Companies often store data in silos, hindering effective analysis, with many relying on outdated systems or paper records.
- Data integration is a complex human problem, where access to data is frequently obstructed by security concerns, inter-departmental politics, or reluctance to change.
- Gaining access to data and ensuring it is clean and standardized requires significant labor and careful negotiation, particularly within large organizations.
- Data cleaning is often labor-intensive and challenging to automate, highlighting the need for human expertise in understanding the context of the data.
- AI integration into corporate structures will proceed cautiously and gradually due to the necessity of convincing organizations of its value and overcoming internal resistance.
- Effective data integration is crucial for AI to provide substantial productivity improvements in industry, demanding a significant time investment.

The article underscores the reality that technological transformation is not instantaneous but rather depends on human negotiation and gradual adaptation.
