## Summary

Splink is an open-source Python package developed by a team of analysts at the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) to address data linking challenges faced by governments globally. Launched in 2019, it has been downloaded over 9 million times and has significantly improved data quality by linking records across various datasets, such as courts, prisons, and probation systems.

Key challenges in data linking include variations in records due to changes in personal information and the absence of unique identifiers, which complicate accurate matching. Traditional methods are often slow and inefficient, taking hours or days to process large datasets.

Splink offers several advantages:
- **Speed**: Links a million records in about one minute.
- **Accuracy**: Supports term frequency adjustments and user-defined fuzzy matching.
- **Scalability**: Can handle over 100 million records.
- **Cross-platform compatibility**: Works on various operating systems and cloud providers.
- **Unsupervised learning**: Does not require training data.
- **Interactive outputs**: Helps users diagnose linkage issues.

The success of Splink is attributed to its open-source nature, which fosters collaboration and continuous improvement through user feedback and contributions from a diverse community. This approach has led to faster implementation, higher accuracy, and reduced costs in data linking efforts. The article advocates for more open-source initiatives to enhance collaboration and break down silos in data analysis.
