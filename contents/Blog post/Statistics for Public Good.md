## Summary

The article discusses the need for official statistics in the UK to better serve the public rather than primarily focusing on government needs. Paul Allin highlights the Royal Statistical Society's (RSS) campaign advocating for "public statistics," which emphasizes a question-based approach to identify societal needs for data. While official statistics like CPI and GDP are crucial for government decision-making, there is a tendency to prioritize government users over the general public. The RSS argues for a broader role of statistics to empower citizens and enhance public engagement with important societal issues. The article calls for a shift in how statistical systems operate, suggesting that the UK Statistics Authority (UKSA) should gather public questions and identify quality data sources to address them. The ultimate goal is to create a public sphere where citizens can engage meaningfully with data and statistics.
