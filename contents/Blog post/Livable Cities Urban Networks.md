## Summary

The article focuses on the concept of livable cities through the lens of urban road networks and their analysis using data visualization tools. It outlines the goal of designing cities with improved livability, exemplified by the "15-minute city" concept. Notably, it aggregates rankings from various sources, highlighting that Zurich ranks as the most livable city based on multiple surveys, followed by Vienna, Copenhagen, Vancouver, and others.

The author discusses their use of ChatGPT for generating color palettes to enhance visualizations and subsequently applies the OSMnx Python package for visualizing urban networks. The visualization employs data from OpenStreetMap to analyze and represent road networks of the twelve most livable cities, showcasing the importance of urban network visuals in transportation and urban planning. 

The key takeaway is the interplay between spatial data science and urban planning in creating livable cities, as well as the use of AI tools in enhancing data visualizations.
