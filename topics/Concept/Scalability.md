Scalability refers to the capability of a system to handle a growing amount of work or its potential to accommodate growth. In the context of data systems and architectures, it signifies how well the system can expand by adding resources (such as more servers, cores, or database instances) to manage increased loads. Effective scalability allows systems to maintain performance as they grow, but has to combat issues like diminishing returns and increased overheads from managing distributed resources.
