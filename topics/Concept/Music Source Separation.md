Music Source Separation refers to the computational methods used to isolate individual sound sources from a mixture of sounds, typically in music audio signals. This process is crucial for applications in music analysis, remixing, and enhancing listening experiences. Techniques can involve both signal processing and machine learning, specifically addressing the challenges associated with differing sound qualities, overlapping frequencies, and varying dynamic ranges in audio signals.
