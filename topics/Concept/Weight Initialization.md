Weight initialization refers to the process of setting the initial values of the weights in a neural network before training. Proper initialization is crucial because it can affect the convergence speed and overall performance of the model. Common strategies include random initialization choices that take into account the number of connections to a neuron. Techniques like Xavier and He initialization adjust the initial weights based on the layer size, which helps to maintain the variance of outputs across layers.
