A computational algorithm, also referred to as Monte Carlo simulations, that relies on repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results. It is widely used to approximate the probability of certain outcomes by running simulations many times over, making it valuable for quantifying uncertainty and solving problems in fields such as physics, finance, and statistics. The method is particularly useful in financial modeling, risk assessment, and decision-making processes where outcomes are influenced by various sources of uncertainty.
