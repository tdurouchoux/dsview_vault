Ensemble learning is a machine learning technique that combines multiple models to improve overall performance, robustness, and generalization. This includes various approaches such as cross-validation ensembles (where models are trained on different folds of a cross-validation split and their predictions are averaged), bagging (bootstrap aggregating), boosting (sequential training focusing on errors), stacking (using predictions of base models as input for a higher-level model), and hill climbing (iteratively adding models to improve validation performance). Ensemble learning is effective in reducing variance, bias, and improving predictions by leveraging the diversity and strengths of different models. It is widely used in applications like classification, regression, and time series forecasting.
