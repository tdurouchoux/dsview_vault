The Mailbox Dashboard Syndrome refers to the phenomenon where dashboards are created simply because data is available, rather than addressing a specific user need or question. These dashboards often lack focus, coherence, and utility, resembling a 'tour of the data' rather than a tool for decision-making. They are typically characterized by an overabundance of charts, KPIs, and visual elements that do not contribute to a clear understanding or actionable insight.
