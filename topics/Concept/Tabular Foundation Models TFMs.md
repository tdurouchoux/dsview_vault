Tabular Foundation Models (TFMs) are pre-trained models designed specifically for tabular data. They leverage the principles of Prior-Data Fitted Networks (PFNs) to enable transfer learning across different tabular tasks. TFMs use synthetic data and transformer architectures to make predictions based on context data, allowing them to adapt to new tasks without the need for traditional training processes. Examples of TFMs include TabPFN and TabICL.
