Tabular Foundation Models (TFMs), also referred to as tabular foundation models, are pre-trained deep learning models designed for tabular data. They leverage generalizable representations learned from diverse tabular datasets or synthetic data, often using transformer architectures and principles like Prior-Data Fitted Networks (PFNs). These models enable transfer learning across different tabular tasks, allowing adaptation to new tasks with minimal task-specific data. Examples include TabPFN, TabICL, and Mitra. While promising, their practical applicability is still constrained by data size limitations and computational costs.
