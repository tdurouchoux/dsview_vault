Structural Causal Models (SCMs) are a framework used to represent and analyze causal relationships between variables in a system. They incorporate nodes representing variables and edges representing causal relationships, along with random functions and distributions to generate synthetic data that reflects real-world complexities, including feature interactions and dependencies. SCMs are particularly useful for creating diverse and scalable datasets for training foundation models, as they can simulate a wide range of data-generating processes and define priors over these processes in applications such as Prior-Data Fitted Networks (PFNs).
