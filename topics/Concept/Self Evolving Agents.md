Self-evolving agents are autonomous systems that improve their reasoning and decision-making capabilities over time through feedback, memory, and iterative interaction with their environment. These agents leverage mechanisms such as reflective feedback, parametric adaptation, and validator-driven corrections to refine their internal policies and strategies. By accumulating experience and adapting to evolving contexts, self-evolving agents can generalize across tasks and environments without requiring full retraining. This capability is critical for achieving lifelong learning and robustness in dynamic, real-world scenarios.
