Black-box optimization refers to optimization techniques where the internal workings of the function being optimized are not known or are too complex to derive explicitly. Instead, the function is treated as a 'black box' that can be evaluated through its inputs and outputs. This approach is particularly beneficial in real-world applications where analytic derivatives may be hard to calculate or where direct evaluation is more feasible, making it applicable to various optimization problems across data science fields.
