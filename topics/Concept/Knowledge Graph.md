A structured representation of knowledge as a graph, where entities (e.g., people, topics, documents) are nodes, and relationships between them are edges. Represented as interconnected triplets of entities and their relationships, knowledge graphs enable efficient querying, reasoning, and inference over complex datasets. They are valuable for applications such as AI agents, decision support systems, semantic search, recommendation systems, and data integration. Knowledge graphs can dynamically evolve to handle changing relationships while maintaining historical context.
