A knowledge graph is a structured representation of facts and relationships, typically organized as nodes (entities) and edges (relationships). Knowledge graphs enable organizations to model complex data relationships in a way that makes retrieval and inference easier, and they enhance search engine results with information gathered from various sources. They are commonly used in semantic search, data integration, and to enrich the contextual understanding of AI systems by connecting disparate pieces of information.
