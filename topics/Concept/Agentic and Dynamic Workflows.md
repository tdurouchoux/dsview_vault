Agentic and Dynamic Workflows refer to automated systems where AI-driven agents or processes dynamically plan, decompose, and execute multi-step tasks in parallel or sequentially. These workflows are designed to handle complex, large-scale projects such as codebase-wide migrations, security audits, or refactoring tasks. They emphasize adaptability, parallel execution, independent verification of results, and long-horizon reasoning, enabling them to operate autonomously in dynamic and uncertain environments. Key features include the ability to break down goals into actionable steps, coordinate tasks, integrate feedback for iterative refinement, and resume progress after interruptions. These workflows are foundational for advanced AI assistants and autonomous systems.
