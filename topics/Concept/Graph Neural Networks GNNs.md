Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are a type of neural network specifically designed to operate on graph structures and their associated data. They leverage the relational information inherent in graph data for tasks such as node classification, link prediction, graph classification, fraud detection, social network analysis, and recommendation systems. GNNs utilize message passing mechanisms to propagate information across the graph, allowing nodes to aggregate features from their local neighbors and update their representations accordingly.
