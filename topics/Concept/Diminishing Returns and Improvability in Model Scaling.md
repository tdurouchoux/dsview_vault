The study of the relationship between computational resource investment (e.g., training time, memory) and the performance gains achieved by AI models. This concept encompasses two key phenomena: 1) Improvability vs. Compute, which analyzes how different models benefit from additional compute, with some showing consistent improvements while others plateau quickly; and 2) Diminishing Returns of Scale, where performance improvements plateau or decrease as model size increases beyond a certain point. Understanding these dynamics helps practitioners optimize resource allocation and avoid inefficient scaling.
