Hierarchical Clustering is a method of cluster analysis that builds a tree-like structure (dendrogram) to represent the relationships between clusters at different levels of granularity. It includes approaches like agglomerative (bottom-up) and divisive (top-down) clustering, where clusters are iteratively merged or split to reveal the underlying structure of the data. This technique is useful for applications such as hierarchical topic modeling or understanding nested relationships within data.
