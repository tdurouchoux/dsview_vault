The Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm is a statistical method used for finding maximum likelihood estimates of parameters in probabilistic models, especially when data are incomplete or have missing values. The algorithm iterates between an expectation step (E-step), where it estimates the hidden variables based on current parameter estimates, and a maximization step (M-step), where it updates the parameter estimates to maximize the expected log-likelihood found in the E-step. The EM algorithm is widely utilized in various applications, including clustering, latent variable models, and various statistical inference problems.
