Inductive bias refers to the set of assumptions that a learning algorithm makes to predict outputs for inputs it has not encountered before. Different models possess distinct inductive biases, influencing how they generalize from training data to unseen data. Understanding inductive bias is crucial for model design, as it drives the performance of algorithms like neural networks versus tree-based models, particularly in terms of their ability to handle noise or irrelevant features.
