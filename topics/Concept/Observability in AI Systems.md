Observability in AI systems, including Large Language Models (LLMs), refers to the ability to monitor, measure, and understand the internal state and behavior of AI models and their outputs. This practice involves tracking metrics, logs, and traces to identify issues such as hallucinations, biases, or performance degradation. Observability is essential for debugging stochastic systems, optimizing performance, and ensuring the reliability of AI models in production. It includes monitoring every LLM call, tool invocation, retrieval step, and user interaction, often facilitated by observability tools that capture hierarchical traces for analysis based on metrics like user, session, cost, latency, or custom metadata.
