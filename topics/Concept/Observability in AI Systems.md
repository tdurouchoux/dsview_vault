Observability in AI systems, including AI agents and Large Language Models (LLMs), refers to the practice of monitoring, logging, and analyzing the behavior and internal state of these systems throughout their lifecycle. This includes tracking user interactions, context retrieval, tool calls, decision-making processes, and model outputs to identify failures, inefficiencies, biases, or compliance risks. Observability tools provide capabilities such as trace visualization, automated evaluation, and real-time monitoring to ensure systems perform as expected, meet quality standards, and adhere to governance policies. It is essential for debugging stochastic systems, optimizing performance, and ensuring the reliability of AI models and agents in production.
