Calibration in machine learning refers to the alignment of a model's confidence in its predictions with their actual accuracy. This process can be applied during or after training (post-hoc calibration) to improve reliability, interpretability, and trustworthiness of the model's outputs. Techniques such as temperature scaling and refinement stopping are often used to refine predictions and enhance performance on validation metrics. Calibration also plays a critical role in reducing hallucinations and improving the evaluation of models, particularly in language models.
