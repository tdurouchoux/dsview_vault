Benign overfitting is a phenomenon in machine learning where a model overfits the training data (i.e., memorizes noise or idiosyncrasies) without negatively impacting its performance on unseen data. Unlike traditional overfitting, which harms generalization, benign overfitting occurs when the distortions caused by memorization are localized to specific regions of the training data. Outside these regions, the model retains its ability to generalize effectively. This concept challenges the classical notion that memorization always leads to poor generalization and highlights the importance of understanding the conditions under which overfitting can be harmless or even beneficial.
