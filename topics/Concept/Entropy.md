Entropy is a fundamental concept in information theory that measures the uncertainty or unpredictability in a set of possible outcomes. In the context of data science, it is used to quantify the amount of information that is missing from a dataset or model. Higher entropy indicates greater disorder or randomness, while lower entropy suggests that the dataset is more predictable or organized. It has applications in various areas including algorithms for machine learning, feature selection, and data compression.
