Round-trip relay simulations are a method used to evaluate the performance of AI systems in delegated document editing tasks. This approach involves a forward edit (e.g., splitting a ledger by person) followed by a backward edit (e.g., merging the ledger files back into a single chronological ledger) to assess whether the AI system can accurately restore the original document. This method is crucial for testing the faithfulness and error-free execution of AI-driven document editing tasks.
