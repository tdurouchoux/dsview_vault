The ARC (Abstraction and Reasoning Challenge) is a benchmark designed to assess machine intelligence by creating a test that is resistant to memorization. It focuses on evaluating the ability to perform tasks that require core knowledge—basic concepts that even young children can understand—rather than relying on the vast memorization of data. Each task is novel, meaning that models cannot simply retrieve answers from memory, but must synthesize solutions on-the-fly.
