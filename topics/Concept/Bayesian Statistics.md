Bayesian Statistics is a subset of statistics in which Bayesian principles are applied to update the probability for a hypothesis as more evidence or information becomes available. It relies on Bayes' theorem and emphasizes the use of prior beliefs or knowledge, allowing for both subjective interpretations and formal statistical modeling. The approach integrates prior distributions, depicting initial beliefs about parameters, with the likelihood of observed data to produce posterior distributions that reflect updated beliefs after the data is accounted for.
