Contextual word embeddings are dynamic representations of words that change based on their surrounding text, as opposed to static embeddings that assign a fixed vector to each word. This allows for an improved understanding of word meaning in varied contexts, thus enhancing the performance of language models in various tasks such as machine translation and sentiment analysis. Techniques such as those employed in transformer models contribute significantly to generating these embeddings.
