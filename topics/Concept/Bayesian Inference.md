Bayesian inference, also referred to as Bayesian statistics, is a method of statistical inference that applies Bayes' theorem to update the probability of a hypothesis as additional evidence or information becomes available. It treats parameters as random variables with probability distributions, combining prior knowledge with observed data to produce posterior distributions. This framework provides a principled way to quantify uncertainty and is particularly useful in scenarios where prior information can be integrated with observed data to make more informed decisions.
