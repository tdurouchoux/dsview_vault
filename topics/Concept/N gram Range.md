N-gram range refers to the span of consecutive words or tokens considered when analyzing or processing text. For example, a unigram (1-gram) considers single words, while a bigram (2-gram) considers pairs of consecutive words. In keyword extraction, the n-gram range determines the length of the resulting keywords or keyphrases. Adjusting the n-gram range allows for flexibility in capturing single words, short phrases, or longer multi-word expressions, depending on the requirements of the task.
