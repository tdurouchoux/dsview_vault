A problem-solving strategy where complex tasks are broken down into smaller, more manageable sub-tasks or subproblems. This approach, which can be applied recursively or systematically, enables efficient handling of intractable inputs or tasks by improving modularity, parallelization, and execution. In contexts such as Reinforcement Learning Models (RLMs) or multi-agent environments, task decomposition facilitates dynamic slicing, delegation, and monitoring of tasks based on attributes like criticality, complexity, and resource constraints.
