Large Reasoning Models (LRMs) refer to sophisticated extensions of traditional language models that incorporate advanced reasoning capabilities, allowing them to solve complex problems by generating detailed thinking processes and intermediate solutions before arriving at a final answer. They represent a shift from mere text generation to a more nuanced approach that enables improved performance on reasoning tasks, including mathematical and logical puzzles, while also highlighting the limitations in model generalization and exact computation.
