Factuality in AI refers to the capability of AI systems, particularly large language models (LLMs), to generate accurate responses based on provided inputs or source material. This concept addresses the challenge of 'hallucinations', where models produce false or misleading information, impacting trust and effectiveness in real-world applications. Evaluating the factual accuracy of AI responses involves comparing them against reliable data sources to ensure that generated answers are grounded in factual correctness.
