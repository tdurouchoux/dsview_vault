Return on Investment (ROI) in Data Teams refers to the challenge of quantifying the value and impact produced by data functions within an organization. It is commonly discussed in the context of data budgeting and resource allocation, where data leaders are asked to justify expenses on tools, manpower, and data infrastructure. Unlike traditional ROI calculations, the value generated by data teams is often intangible, making it challenging to link specific outputs directly to financial gains. This concept emphasizes the need for a qualitative understanding of value through stakeholder feedback, advocacy, and the importance of perceived usefulness in different business functions.
