Dimensionality reduction is a technique used to reduce the number of input variables in datasets while retaining important information. This transformation simplifies models and visualizations, makes computations more efficient, and mitigates the curse of dimensionality. Methods such as Principal Component Analysis (PCA), t-SNE, and UMAP are commonly employed to achieve dimensionality reduction in various fields, including Data Science, for exploratory data analysis and visualization.
