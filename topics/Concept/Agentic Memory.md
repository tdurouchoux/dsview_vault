Agentic memory refers to the dynamic and structured storage, retrieval, and utilization of information to support reasoning, planning, and decision-making in autonomous agents. Unlike traditional memory systems, agentic memory integrates feedback loops, structured representations (e.g., graphs, workflows), and active management policies to ensure relevance and coherence over extended interactions. It enables agents to accumulate knowledge, adapt strategies, and maintain consistency across tasks, making it a critical component of self-evolving and collective reasoning systems.
