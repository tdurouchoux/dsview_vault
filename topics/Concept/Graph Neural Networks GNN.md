Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are a class of neural networks specifically designed to process data structured as graphs. They leverage the relationships and connections among nodes to learn representations that encode the underlying graph structure. GNNs can perform tasks like node classification, link prediction, graph classification, and fraud detection by aggregating information from neighboring nodes and utilizing message passing mechanisms to capture complex relational dynamics within graph data.
