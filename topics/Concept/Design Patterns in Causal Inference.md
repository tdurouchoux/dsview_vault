Design patterns in causal inference refer to established methodologies and frameworks that guide researchers in addressing causal questions and designing studies effectively. These patterns encapsulate best practices for experimental and observational study designs, helping to mitigate biases, improve validity, and ensure robust causal conclusions. They serve as a blueprint for structuring research questions, data collection, and analysis in a manner that enhances the reliability of causal estimates.
