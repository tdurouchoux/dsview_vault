Deanonymization refers to the process of re-identifying individuals from pseudonymous or anonymized data. This is achieved by matching micro-data (e.g., unique attributes, writing style, interests, or incidental disclosures) extracted from unstructured text (such as online posts, interviews, or reviews) against a database of known identities or candidate profiles. Unlike traditional anonymization techniques that rely on structured data (e.g., movie ratings, spatiotemporal points), modern deanonymization leverages advanced tools like Large Language Models (LLMs) and semantic embeddings to automate the extraction, search, and reasoning steps at scale. The goal is to link anonymous accounts to real identities, consolidate a user’s activity across platforms, or identify users based on seemingly innocuous data. Deanonymization poses significant privacy risks, as it invalidates the assumption that pseudonymous participation online provides adequate protection. Techniques for deanonymization include profile extraction, embedding-based search, LLM reasoning for candidate verification, and confidence calibration to control false positive rates. This concept is foundational in fields like cybersecurity, privacy research, and LLM safety evaluations.
