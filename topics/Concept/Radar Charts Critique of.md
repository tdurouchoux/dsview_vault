Radar charts, also known as spider charts or web charts, are a graphical method for displaying multivariate data in the form of a two-dimensional chart with axes radiating from a central point. While they can be visually appealing, radar charts have significant limitations, particularly when used to represent independent metrics without a natural angular structure. Critiques highlight issues such as arbitrary axis ordering, area distortion, and difficulties in comparing non-adjacent metrics, which can mislead interpretation.
