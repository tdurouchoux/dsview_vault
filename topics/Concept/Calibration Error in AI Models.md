Calibration Error refers to the discrepancy between the predicted probability scores of a model and the actual outcomes. A well-calibrated model provides accurate confidence estimates for its predictions. In the context of AI, particularly for LLMs tested against benchmarks like the Humanity's Last Exam, assessing calibration is essential to evaluate how confidently models provide answers and to mitigate issues like hallucinations or incorrect assertions.
