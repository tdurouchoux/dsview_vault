Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) is a paradigm in machine learning where the model learns representations from the data itself without relying on explicit human-labeled data. By leveraging inherent structures or relationships within the data, SSL creates pretext tasks (e.g., predicting missing words in a sentence for natural language processing or predicting image rotations for computer vision) to extract meaningful features. This approach reduces dependency on annotated datasets and has gained significant attention in fields like computer vision and natural language processing due to its ability to utilize large amounts of unlabeled data for downstream tasks such as classification or regression.
