Open Source Licenses are legal frameworks that govern how software can be used, modified, and shared. They are critical in the open-source community, allowing developers to collaborate and innovate while protecting intellectual property. Different types of licenses, such as permissive or copyleft licenses, can significantly affect how software and AI models are utilized and distributed in the ecosystem, fostering community development and sustainability.
