Critical thinking in data analysis refers to the objective evaluation of data and visualizations to make reasoned judgments based on evidence. It involves questioning assumptions, evaluating the credibility of sources, scrutinizing methodologies, and considering alternative explanations to ensure that analyses are sound and conclusions are valid. This skill is essential for data scientists to avoid pitfalls associated with biased or misleading representations.
