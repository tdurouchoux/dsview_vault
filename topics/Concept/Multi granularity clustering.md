Multi-granularity clustering refers to the process of generating multiple layers of clusters with varying levels of granularity from a single dataset. In this context, it involves creating a hierarchy of clusters where the first layer represents the finest-grained clusters, and subsequent layers represent increasingly coarser-grained clusters. This approach is particularly useful for applications like topic modeling, where different levels of detail are required. Multi-granularity clustering allows users to zoom in or out of the data to explore clusters at different resolutions, providing flexibility in analysis.
