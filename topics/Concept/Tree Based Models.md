Tree-based models are a type of machine learning algorithm that uses a tree-like model of decisions and their possible consequences. They are often utilized for both classification and regression tasks and include algorithms such as Decision Trees, Random Forests, and Gradient Boosting Machines (e.g., XGBoost). These models are particularly effective on structured and tabular data, providing interpretability and robustness against overfitting, especially on datasets with fewer samples.
