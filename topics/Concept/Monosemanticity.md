Monosemanticity refers to the property of features in a machine learning model being interpretable as representing a single, distinct concept or idea. This concept contrasts with polysemantic features, which can relate to multiple concepts or meanings. In the context of neural networks and specifically transformer models, achieving monosemantic representations simplifies interpretability and facilitates understanding how models derive outputs from specific inputs.
