Model Fusion refers to the technique of combining the outputs of multiple machine learning models or large language models (LLMs) to produce a single, more accurate, or comprehensive result. This approach leverages the strengths of diverse models to improve performance on complex tasks, such as reasoning, tool usage, and synthesis of information. Fusion can involve parallel execution of models, followed by aggregation or adjudication of their outputs using a judge model or a predefined set of rules. This method is particularly useful in scenarios where no single model excels across all dimensions of a task, enabling a more robust and nuanced response.
