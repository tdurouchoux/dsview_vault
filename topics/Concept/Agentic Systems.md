Agentic systems, also referred to as agentic reasoning, represent architectures or paradigms where large language models (LLMs) are framed as autonomous agents capable of planning, acting, and learning through continual interaction with their environment. These systems emphasize dynamic decision-making, tool use, and adaptation to open-ended and evolving environments, bridging the gap between abstract thought and concrete action. Agentic systems can be structured as predefined workflows or dynamic agents, aiming to reduce manual effort, accelerate task accomplishment, and improve collaboration by automating or semi-automating complex tasks. This concept is foundational to modern AI systems that integrate reasoning, execution, and self-improvement into a unified framework.
