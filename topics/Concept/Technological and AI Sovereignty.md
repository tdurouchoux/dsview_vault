Technological and AI sovereignty refers to a region's or entity's ability to independently develop, control, and deploy critical technologies, including AI, without excessive reliance on external actors. This concept emphasizes reducing dependencies on foreign technologies, ensuring data security, and promoting homegrown innovation. It is central to initiatives like national AI strategies, state-backed investments in AI supercomputing, and broader efforts to strengthen autonomy in digital infrastructure and AI ecosystems.
