Software supply chain security, also referred to as supply-chain security, encompasses the practices and measures taken to protect the integrity and security of software and hardware components, dependencies, and infrastructure throughout the development, distribution, and deployment processes. This includes safeguarding against threats such as tampering, counterfeiting, or the insertion of malicious components, ensuring that third-party libraries, tools, and systems are free from vulnerabilities or backdoors that could be exploited by attackers.
