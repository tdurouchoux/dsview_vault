Typography in data visualization involves the strategic selection and application of typefaces to organize and present data clearly. This includes considerations of font styles (serif vs. sans-serif), font sizes, weights, and the use of figures (lining vs. oldstyle, proportional vs. tabular) that enhance data interpretability. The choice of typography impacts user perception, comprehension, and the overall aesthetic of visual data representation.
