Model and Inference Optimization refers to the process of improving the efficiency, speed, and performance of machine learning models during both training and deployment. This includes techniques such as quantization (e.g., GGUF models), pruning, distillation, and leveraging tools like llama.cpp to reduce computational overhead, memory usage, or latency. These optimizations are critical for enabling faster local inference, scalable deployment, and improved overall model performance.
