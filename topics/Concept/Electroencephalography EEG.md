Electroencephalography (EEG) is a technique used to record electrical activity of the brain through electrodes placed on the scalp. It provides insights into brain function by capturing electrical signals resulting from neuronal activity. EEG is non-invasive and widely utilized in clinical and research settings for diagnosing conditions such as epilepsy, sleep disorders, and more recently in developing brain-computer interfaces.
