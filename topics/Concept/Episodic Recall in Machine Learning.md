Episodic recall refers to the ability of a model to store and retrieve specific instances or experiences from training data, rather than relying solely on abstracted patterns or generalizations. This concept is inspired by human memory systems, where episodic memory stores veridical experiences that can be recalled later. In machine learning, episodic recall can support generalization by enabling models to reuse specific instances for solving novel tasks, particularly when the abstractions learned during training discard information that might be useful for future tasks. Recent research suggests that explicit episodic recall mechanisms may be necessary for unlocking certain types of generalization.
