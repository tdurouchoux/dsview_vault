Structural Causal Models (SCMs) are frameworks used to represent causal relationships between variables in a system. They consist of nodes representing variables and edges representing causal relationships. SCMs are used to generate synthetic data and define priors over data-generating processes in Prior-Data Fitted Networks (PFNs). By sampling from SCMs, models can learn to make predictions based on the underlying causal structure of the data.
