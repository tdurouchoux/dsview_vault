Red teaming in AI involves simulating adversarial attacks or identifying vulnerabilities in AI systems to assess their robustness, security, and reliability. This process is conducted by both internal and external teams to proactively test AI models for weaknesses, potential biases, jailbreaks, and other risks. It includes adversary simulations and a sociotechnical approach to identify and mitigate risks before deployment, ensuring the AI system can handle real-world challenges and maintain performance under stress. Red teaming is a form of adversarial testing that helps improve the safeguards and overall resilience of AI systems.
