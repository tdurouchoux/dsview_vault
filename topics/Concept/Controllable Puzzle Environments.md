Controllable puzzle environments are specially designed settings used to evaluate the reasoning capabilities of models by systematically manipulating problem complexity. By providing precise control over the challenges faced (such as the number of elements or complexity of tasks), these environments allow for thorough testing of models' reasoning processes and the validity of their solutions, ensuring that evaluations are not influenced by data contamination typically found in established benchmarks.
