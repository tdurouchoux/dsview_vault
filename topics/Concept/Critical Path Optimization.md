Critical path optimization refers to the process of identifying and reducing the longest delay path in a hardware design, which determines the maximum clock frequency at which the circuit can operate. In Talos V2, optimizations such as folding operations, bounding math engines, and reducing serial bottlenecks are employed to shorten the critical path and improve timing closure. This ensures that the design can run at higher clock frequencies, thereby increasing throughput.
