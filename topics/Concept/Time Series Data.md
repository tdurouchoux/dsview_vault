Time-Series Data refers to a sequence of data points collected or recorded at successive points in time. This type of data is ordered chronologically and is often used to analyze trends, patterns, and anomalies over time. Time-series data can be univariate, consisting of a single variable measured over time, or multivariate, involving multiple variables. It is widely used in various fields such as finance, healthcare, environmental science, and engineering to monitor and analyze dynamic systems.
