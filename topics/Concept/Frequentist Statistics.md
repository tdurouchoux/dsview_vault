Frequentist Statistics is a paradigm of statistical inference that relies on the frequency or proportion of data. It involves hypothesis testing and focus on outcomes in terms of the long-run frequency of events, and does not utilize prior distributions. This approach generally emphasizes confidence intervals and p-values as primary statistics for decision making, distinguishing it sharply from Bayesian methods that allow for prior knowledge. Frequently, this paradigm is associated with null hypothesis significance testing.
