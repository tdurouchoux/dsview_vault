Fuzzy Deduplication, also referred to as automatic duplicate detection, is the process of identifying and removing duplicate or near-duplicate items within a dataset based on a similarity threshold. This technique is widely used for data cleaning, deduplication, or identifying redundant information, particularly in large datasets where manual inspection is impractical. It is crucial for tasks like information retrieval, text clustering, and training high-quality datasets for Large Language Models (LLMs), as it helps avoid performance overestimation, wasted compute, and overestimation of feature importance by ensuring the uniqueness and relevance of the data.
