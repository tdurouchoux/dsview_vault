Reranking is a method used to refine and improve the relevance of retrieved documents in information retrieval systems, such as search engines or retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines. It involves using specialized models, often called reranking models or cross-encoders, to reassess and reorder the results of an initial retrieval process. These models consider deeper contextual or semantic relationships between the query and documents, enabling the rearrangement of items to maximize their relevance. Reranking is typically applied after an initial search to enhance precision, such as selecting the top results from a broader set of candidates for further processing or consumption.
