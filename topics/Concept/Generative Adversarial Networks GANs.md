Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) are a class of machine learning frameworks that consist of two neural networks, a generator and a discriminator, that contest with each other to create data indistinguishable from real data. Initially designed for tasks such as generating images, GANs have also been adapted for anomaly detection by leveraging their ability to learn distributions of training data and generate realistic samples.
