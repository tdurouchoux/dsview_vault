Open standards are publicly available specifications that define protocols, formats, or methodologies to ensure interoperability, compatibility, and consistency across different systems and organizations. In the context of data architecture, open standards enable seamless data exchange, integration, and processing by providing vendor-neutral guidelines for contracts, schemas, storage, movement, transformation, discovery, and governance. They are typically governed by independent foundations or communities, ensuring transparency and avoiding vendor lock-in.
