Adversarial robustness refers to the ability of machine learning models to maintain performance when subjected to adversarial attacks, such as those designed to manipulate or exploit vulnerabilities in the model's design. It is a critical area of research, particularly in the context of large language models, where the goal is to defend against malicious prompts that seek to generate harmful or undesired outputs.
