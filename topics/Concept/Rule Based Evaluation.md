Rule-Based Evaluation is a deterministic approach to assessing the performance of tools or generated outputs by comparing them against predefined rules, ground-truth datasets, or domain-specific criteria. This method ensures consistency, transparency, and reproducibility, making it ideal for benchmarking, quality control, and domain-specific applications. Rule-based evaluation can assess various aspects of outputs, such as syntax correctness, format adherence, language accuracy, and keyword presence, and is often tailored to specific tasks or requirements.
