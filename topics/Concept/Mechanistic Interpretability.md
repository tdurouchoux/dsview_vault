Mechanistic interpretability is a field of research aimed at understanding the internal workings of machine learning models, particularly neural networks. It focuses on revealing the specific mechanisms and processes that govern how models operate, thereby transforming them from opaque 'black boxes' into systems whose decisions and behaviors can be understood and predicted. This area of study has gained momentum given the rising complexity of AI systems and the associated risks that arise from their lack of transparency.
