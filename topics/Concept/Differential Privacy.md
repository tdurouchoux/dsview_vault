Differential Privacy is a technique used to enhance the privacy of individuals in datasets. It involves introducing randomness or noise to the data or the results of data analyses to ensure that the presence or absence of a single data point does not significantly affect the output. In the context of federated learning, differential privacy helps to prevent the reconstruction of individual user data from the global model, providing an additional layer of privacy assurance.
