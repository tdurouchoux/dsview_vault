Masked Autoencoders (MAE) are a type of self-supervised learning model that learns to reconstruct missing or masked portions of input data. In the context of images, MAE divides an image into patches, masks a significant portion of these patches, and trains the model to reconstruct the masked patches. This approach encourages the model to learn meaningful representations of the data by focusing on the relationships between visible and masked patches.
