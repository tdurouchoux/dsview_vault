Optimization biases are tendencies in the training process (e.g., gradient-based optimization) that favor learning certain types of solutions over others. For instance, models trained with gradient descent often learn linear structures before nonlinear ones, and simpler representations tend to dominate even as more complex features are learned later. These biases arise because simpler solutions are often easier to optimize and generalize better, leading to faster and more consistent learning. Optimization biases play a critical role in explaining why models generalize well despite having the capacity to memorize training data.
