Cosine similarity is a metric used to measure how similar two vectors are in an inner product space. It is defined as the cosine of the angle between the two vectors and ranges from -1 to 1. High values (close to 1) indicate that the two vectors are pointing in the same direction, while values close to 0 indicate orthogonality, and negative values indicate that the vectors point in opposite directions. It is often used in various fields, including natural language processing and information retrieval, to compare items based on their vector representations. However, its application can sometimes yield misleading results, especially if it is used without proper consideration of the underlying data representation.
