AI Agents, also referred to as Personal AI Agents, are autonomous or semi-autonomous systems that leverage artificial intelligence to plan, execute, and manage tasks on behalf of users. These agents can operate as assistants, coworkers, or coaches, performing a wide range of functions such as managing emails, calendars, automating workflows, data gathering, market research, and customer account management. 

Characterized by their ability to understand user intent, interact with digital environments, and retain context over time (persistent memory), AI agents integrate seamlessly with various platforms and tools. They operate across multiple interfaces like chat apps (e.g., WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord) and extend their functionality through plugins or custom skills. 

AI agents range from simple chatbots to complex systems capable of autonomous planning, learning, and task execution. They use language models to reason about their environment and interact with external tools or functions, often operating through a cycle of thoughts, actions, and observations. Frameworks like LangGraph, LlamaIndex, and smolagents are commonly used to streamline their development. 

These agents are particularly useful for open-ended problems where the required steps are unpredictable, making them ideal for scaling tasks in trusted environments. They can operate independently for private, offline tasks or as part of larger connected systems, handling common commands at the edge and routing more complex tasks to larger models. Extensive testing and appropriate guardrails are essential for their reliable deployment.
