Misleading visualizations occur when the design of charts and graphs intentionally or unintentionally distorts the data they represent, leading to erroneous interpretations. This can happen through techniques such as selective data representation, inappropriate scaling, or manipulation of axes. Recognizing the signs of misleading visualizations is crucial for data scientists and consumers of data to draw accurate conclusions from visualized information.
