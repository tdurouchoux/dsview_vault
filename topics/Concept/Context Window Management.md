The practice of managing and optimizing the context window of AI models and tools to ensure critical information is preserved and efficiently utilized. This includes strategies such as context compaction (summarizing or offloading older context), tool call offloading, skills (progressive disclosure of tools), leveraging persistent artifacts (e.g., plan.md), auto-compaction features, curating and organizing context into structured formats (e.g., persistent files, knowledge graphs), and dynamically adjusting the context to maintain relevance and avoid performance degradation. Effective context window management enables long, uninterrupted interactions and ensures AI systems focus on the most pertinent details.
