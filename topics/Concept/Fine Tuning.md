Fine-tuning is a transfer learning technique where a pre-trained neural network or AI model is further trained on a smaller, task-specific dataset to adapt it to a particular application. This process involves adjusting the model's parameters, often by unfreezing higher-level layers and retraining them with a lower learning rate, to leverage general features learned from the original dataset while capturing the nuances of the new data. Fine-tuning is essential for specializing general models in specific domains, such as chat models or domain-specific tasks, and is often evaluated using benchmarks to ensure quality. Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) is a specific process where a pre-trained language model is further trained on a labeled dataset to adapt to specific tasks, learn to structure answers, and reactivate a subset of knowledge learned during pre-training.
