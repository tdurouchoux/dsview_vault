Agentic coding, also referred to as AI coding agents, involves autonomous or semi-autonomous systems powered by large language models (LLMs) that perform coding tasks such as writing, debugging, refactoring, and architectural design. These systems, or agents, can autonomously reason over codebases, interact with external tools, execute multi-step tasks, and iterate on solutions with minimal human supervision. Agentic coding tasks are characterized by long-horizon interactions, repeated context ingestion, and tool-driven exploration, which contribute to their high token consumption and variability. They represent a significant advancement in AI's ability to assist or automate software development and security processes, often operating in closed-loop systems and leveraging tool-use protocols.
