Autonomous or semi-autonomous software agents, often powered by large language models (LLMs), designed to perform coding tasks such as writing, debugging, refactoring, and architectural design. These agents interact with development environments, execute tools (e.g., reading/writing files, running shell commands), manage sessions, and maintain transcripts of their actions. They operate by processing instructions, reasoning over codebases, and leveraging modular architectures to separate concerns like decision-making, environment interaction, and user interface. Agentic coding tasks are characterized by long-horizon interactions, repeated context ingestion, and tool-driven exploration, contributing to their high token consumption and variability. These systems represent a significant advancement in AI's ability to assist or automate software development and security processes, often operating in closed-loop systems and leveraging tool-use protocols.
