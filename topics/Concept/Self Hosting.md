Self-hosting refers to the practice of deploying and managing software applications on infrastructure owned or controlled by the user, rather than relying on third-party cloud services. In the context of LLM engineering platforms like Langfuse, self-hosting allows organizations to maintain full control over their data, ensure compliance with regulatory frameworks, and customize the platform to meet specific operational or security requirements. Self-hosting options include Docker Compose, Kubernetes (Helm), and cloud deployments via AWS, GCP, or Azure.
