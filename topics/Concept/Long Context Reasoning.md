Long context reasoning, often associated with Long-Context Large Language Models (LLMs), refers to the ability of a model to process, decompose, and aggregate information from inputs significantly larger than traditional context windows. These models are designed to handle extensive textual inputs, enabling tasks such as document comprehension, multi-session interactions, and complex problem-solving where information is distributed across a large input space. Long-context LLMs and related paradigms, like RLMs, have demonstrated superior performance in benchmarks such as Oolong and LongCoT, showcasing their capability to manage and reason over inputs that exceed traditional context limits.
