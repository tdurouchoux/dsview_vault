Agent capabilities in the context of AI refer to the functionalities that allow an AI model, typically a large language model, to perform tasks autonomously. This includes making decisions based on predefined rules or learned behaviors, interacting with external tools (e.g., performing a web search or executing commands), and adapting to various environments and tasks. Incorporating agent capabilities into AI systems enhances their utility and versatility in practical applications.
