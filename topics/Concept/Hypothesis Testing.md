A statistical method used to make decisions about a population parameter based on sample data, also known as Null Hypothesis Significance Testing (NHST). It involves formulating a null hypothesis and an alternative hypothesis, calculating a test statistic (e.g., p-value), and determining whether to reject the null hypothesis based on the significance of the observed data. This process helps researchers distinguish signals from noise in empirical data and safeguards against confirmation bias by ensuring claims are made with a controlled error rate.
