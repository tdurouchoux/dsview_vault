Agentic planning, also referred to as planning in AI agents, is the process of decomposing goals, generating sequences of actions, and navigating complex environments to achieve specific objectives. It involves foundational capabilities like in-context planning, post-training planning, and structured workflows, often leveraging techniques such as tree search or reinforcement learning. Agentic planning emphasizes adaptability, long-horizon reasoning, and the integration of feedback to refine strategies over time. It is a cornerstone of autonomous agents, enabling them to operate effectively in dynamic and uncertain environments by generating, validating, and executing plans.
