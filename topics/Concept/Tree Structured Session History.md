Tree-structured session history is a method of storing and managing the conversation or interaction history of an AI system in a branching tree format. This allows users to navigate to any previous point in the session, create branches for different lines of inquiry, and continue the interaction from there. In Pi, this feature enables users to explore multiple paths in a conversation, bookmark important entries, and export or share specific branches. It provides flexibility and control over how the interaction history is utilized and revisited.
