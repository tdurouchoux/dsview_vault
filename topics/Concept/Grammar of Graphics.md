The Grammar of Graphics is a conceptual framework and system for understanding, creating, and building data visualizations based on a set of grammatical rules. It defines a structured and modular approach to constructing plots by breaking them down into fundamental components, such as data, aesthetics, geometries, scales, and themes. This framework enables the creation of complex and customized visualizations in a systematic and declarative way, often implemented in tools like ggplot2 in R.
