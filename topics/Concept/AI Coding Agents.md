AI coding agents, also referred to as coding agents, are autonomous or semi-autonomous systems powered by large language models (LLMs) that generate, test, and refine code. These agents can interact with external tools, execute code, and iterate on solutions with minimal human intervention. They operate in closed-loop systems, dynamically performing actions, evaluating outcomes, and adjusting their approach. AI coding agents are designed to handle complex coding tasks such as debugging, refactoring, and architectural design, often leveraging tool-use protocols like the Model Context Protocol (MCP). Examples include Claude Code, Codex CLI, and Gemini CLI, which enable both synchronous and asynchronous coding tasks.
