AI coding agents, also referred to as agentic coding, are autonomous or semi-autonomous systems powered by large language models (LLMs) that perform coding tasks such as writing, debugging, refactoring, and architectural design. These agents can autonomously reason over codebases, interact with external tools, execute multi-step tasks, and iterate on solutions with minimal human intervention. They represent a significant advancement in AI's ability to assist or automate software development and security processes, often operating in closed-loop systems and leveraging tool-use protocols like the Model Context Protocol (MCP).
