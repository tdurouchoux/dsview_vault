Continuous-Time Markov Chains (CTMCs) are stochastic processes characterized by the transitions between states at any point in time, differentiating from discrete-time Markov chains where state changes only occur at specified intervals. In CTMCs, the waiting time in a state follows an exponential distribution, which retains the memoryless property, allowing for complex modeling of systems where events happen continuously over time.
