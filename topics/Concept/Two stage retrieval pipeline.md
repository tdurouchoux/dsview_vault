A two-stage retrieval pipeline is a popular framework in information retrieval where the first stage efficiently retrieves a small set of candidate documents using a lightweight method, and the second stage re-ranks these documents using a more complex, often deep learning-based model to maximize retrieval performance. This approach balances performance and efficiency, leveraging the strengths of both simple and sophisticated models.
