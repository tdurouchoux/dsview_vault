Summarization evaluation metrics are quantitative measures used to assess the quality of generated summaries. These metrics include traditional methods like ROUGE, which evaluates the overlap of n-grams between generated and reference texts, and BERTScore, which utilizes contextual embeddings to capture semantic similarities beyond mere word overlap. Additionally, newer approaches leverage large language models (LLMs) as evaluators, providing reference-free assessments based on fluency, coherence, relevance, and factual consistency.
