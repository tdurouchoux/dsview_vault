Benchmark misclassification errors occur when a benchmark incorrectly evaluates a model's performance due to flaws in its evaluation criteria or methods. These errors are categorized into two types: (1) False Positives, where incorrect solutions are rewarded due to incomplete or flawed verification processes, and (2) False Negatives, where correct solutions are penalized due to overly rigid or specific test conditions. Reducing misclassification errors is critical for ensuring accurate and reliable benchmarking.
