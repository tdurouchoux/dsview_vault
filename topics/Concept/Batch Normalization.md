Batch normalization is a technique to improve the training of deep neural networks. It normalizes the inputs of a layer by adjusting and scaling them based on the mean and variance of the current mini-batch, effectively standardizing the level of input across different layers. This can lead to faster convergence during training, reduces sensitivity to initialization, and can improve model performance by allowing for larger learning rates. It is typically applied between the linear transformation and the non-linear activation function.
