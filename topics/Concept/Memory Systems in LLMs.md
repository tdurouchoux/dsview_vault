Memory systems in large language models (LLMs) refer to mechanisms that enable models to retain, update, and retrieve information across extended interactions or sessions. Unlike traditional retrieval-based memory, RLMs and related approaches treat memory as an emergent property of recursive processing, where the model dynamically inspects, partitions, and re-queries its input context to simulate memory-like behavior. Benchmarks like LongMemEval evaluate these systems on tasks such as multi-session reasoning, temporal reasoning, and knowledge updates, demonstrating their potential as alternatives to classical retrieval stacks.
