Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) are computational frameworks where multiple specialized agents collaborate or compete to achieve common goals. In data science, these systems involve agents with distinct roles such as planning, coding, verification, debugging, and report writing. Agents work together through structured communication protocols, shared memory systems, and coordination strategies to solve complex tasks. MAS emphasize orchestration, coordination, and management to handle long-horizon tasks, leveraging collective intelligence, trust mechanisms, and adaptive execution for efficient, reliable, and scalable problem-solving in decentralized environments.
