Performance metrics are quantitative measures used to assess the efficiency, speed, and effectiveness of a computing system or algorithm. In the context of big data systems, metrics such as COST (Configuration that Outperforms a Single Thread) provide insights into hardware and software efficiencies by comparing the performance of distributed systems to single-threaded implementations. This helps gauge whether the scalability of a system translates into actual performance gains, highlighting overhead versus true compute power.
