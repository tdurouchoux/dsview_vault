VGG16 is a convolutional neural network architecture developed by Karen Simonyan and Andrew Zisserman from the University of Oxford. It is known for its simplicity and effectiveness in image classification tasks. The model consists of 16 layers with small 3x3 convolutional filters and max-pooling layers, making it a deep and powerful network for feature extraction and transfer learning. VGG16 was trained on the ImageNet dataset, which contains over 14 million images across 1,000 classes, making it highly effective for a wide range of computer vision tasks.
