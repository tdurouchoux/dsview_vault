LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) is a type of recurrent neural network architecture that is particularly effective for sequence prediction tasks. LSTM networks are designed to overcome the limitations of traditional RNNs by introducing memory cells that can maintain information over long periods, effectively learning from sequences of data with variable lengths. They are widely used in applications such as time series forecasting, natural language processing, and speech recognition. LSTMs employ gating mechanisms to control the flow of information, allowing the network to learn what to remember or forget.
