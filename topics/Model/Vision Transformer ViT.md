Vision Transformer (ViT) is a deep learning model architecture that applies the transformer architecture, originally designed for natural language processing, to image classification and other computer vision tasks. It processes images by dividing them into fixed-size patches, linearly embedding these patches, and feeding them into transformer layers. ViT leverages self-attention mechanisms to capture global dependencies in images and has demonstrated strong performance and scalability, particularly when pre-trained on large datasets. It is widely used in state-of-the-art computer vision models and applications such as AI weather prediction systems.
