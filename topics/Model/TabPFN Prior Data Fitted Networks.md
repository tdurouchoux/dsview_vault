TabPFN (Tabular Prior-data Fitted Networks) is a tabular foundation model and framework that leverages Prior-Data Fitted Networks (PFNs) to enable in-context learning for tabular data. It is pre-trained on synthetic and real data, allowing it to make predictions without explicit training. TabPFN is designed to handle small to medium-sized tabular datasets efficiently and is noted for its strong performance with minimal tuning. The model uses a transformer architecture to sample tasks and datasets, integrating over latent tasks to enable transfer learning across different tabular problems.
