ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) is a class of statistical models used for analyzing and forecasting time series data. The model relies on three key parameters: p, d, and q, which represent the order of the autoregressive term, the degree of differencing needed to achieve stationarity, and the order of the moving average term, respectively. ARIMA is particularly useful for univariate time series data and requires the series to be stationary, which often necessitates preprocessing to eliminate trends and seasonality.
