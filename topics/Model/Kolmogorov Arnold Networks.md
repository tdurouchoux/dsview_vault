Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) are a novel type of neural network architecture that leverage the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem for function approximation. Unlike traditional Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) that apply activation functions at the nodes, KANs apply them on the edges, allowing for potentially superior model accuracy and interpretability, particularly in scenarios involving scientific computations.
