KeyBERT is a Python library designed for keyword extraction and keyphrase extraction from text documents. It leverages BERT embeddings and cosine similarity to identify the most relevant words or phrases that best describe the content of a document. Unlike traditional methods such as TF-IDF or RAKE, KeyBERT uses pre-trained BERT models to generate document and word embeddings, enabling it to capture semantic meanings and contextual relationships in the text. The library is optimized for ease of use, requiring minimal code (e.g., a few lines) and no training from scratch, making it accessible for beginners and efficient for quick implementations.
